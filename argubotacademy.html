Client: Glasslab Games - Redwood City, CA
Project: Mars Generation One: Argubot Academy
Role: Environment Design, Illustration
Tools: Pencil & Paper, Illustrator


Mars Generation One: Argubot Academy is a tablet-based educational game targeted at middle-school aged kids developed by Glasslab Games. In the game, players build and fight robots (known as argubots) against other robot opponents. Players are taught to equip their argubots with logical, well-reasoned arguments to use as "attacks" in a battle of wits with their opponents. I worked as an environment designer on the project. I was responsible for delivering several backgrounds and secondary robot designs set on a sci-fi base established on Mars.

Each environment started out as a small thumbnail sketch. Approved thumbnails were then further developed in pencils. Finally, the approved concept drawings were taken into Adobe Illustrator and developed into the vector artwork that appears within the final game.


Early Dormitory Split View - Here you get a peek behind the curtain to see the amount of vector assets created for one of these environments.


This project was an incredible opportunity to work directly with the Art Director at Glasslab Games. Each environment went through a process of feedback and critique. After a few rounds of this feedback, we arrived at the final result you see in game.

Final "Atrium" envirnoment.


Here we see the kitchen environment with it's many robot helpers taking care of the day-to-day operations of the base such as food growing, harvesting, preparation, and general cleaning. I designed these secondary kitchen bots to fit into the established visual style of the project. The common element between them being thier camera lens like eye. I chose soft rounded forms for the bodies to empasize their friendly and approachable personalities.

The workshop environment, where Argubots are assembled and tested before combat.

Library Enivironment Concept (not used in final game)

I really loved working on this project. Working under the art direction at Glasslab Games was an invaluable learning experience. The importance of exploring ideas with very simple thumbnail sketches cannot be overstated. This approach allowed me to iterate very quickly on ideas and get approval from the Art Director at each stage of development before investing the time it takes to create the final artwork for use within the game. A process which I continue to use in all of my designs.
